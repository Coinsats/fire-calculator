💡 What is Step-up SIP?
📌 Flat SIP (Step-up = 0%)
You invest the same amount every month, forever.
Month 1–60: 10,000/mo
Total invested in 5 yrs: 600,000
Simple. Predictable. But ignores salary growth.
🚀 Step-up SIP (e.g. 10%/yr)
SIP increases every year as your income grows.
Yr 1: 10,000 → Yr 2: 11,000 → Yr 3: 12,100 → ...
Total invested in 5 yrs: ~733,000
Invest more as you earn more. Corpus grows much faster.
How to use this calculator
  1. Enter your starting monthly SIP — what you can invest today.
  2. Set Investment Duration — how many years you plan to stay invested.
  3. Set Annual Return (CAGR) — expected yearly growth rate of your investment.
  4. Set Step-up % — how much you'll increase your SIP every year (matches salary hike).
  5. The chart shows Step-up Corpus vs Flat SIP Corpus so you can see the difference.

💡 How Inflation Adjustment Works
✅ Inflation = 0%
Your withdrawal stays flat forever.
e.g. 10,000/mo every month for 10 years
Total = 10,000 × 120 = 1,200,000
Matches tools like Groww — simple fixed withdrawal.
⚡ Inflation = 5%
Withdrawal increases every year to maintain purchasing power.
Year 1: 10,000 → Year 2: 10,500 → Year 3: 11,025 → ...
By Year 10 you're withdrawing ~15,513/mo
More realistic — accounts for rising cost of living.
Why does this matter? 10,000 AED today won't buy the same things in 5 years. At 5% inflation, you'd need ~12,763 AED in 5 years to maintain the same lifestyle. The inflation adjustment automatically steps up your withdrawal each year so your real spending power stays the same — not just the number.
